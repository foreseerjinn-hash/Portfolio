My name's J, and I'm a student of software design. I'm new to coding, I like it, and want to learn to do it more...

...I began learning in 2025, with the initial intention of exploring more analogue web design.

I was motivated by the "days of old" when everyone and their mum had a website, and knew the basics of coding.
I began asking myself, “What if we could just do this again? What if we didn't have to rely on Meta to feel connected?”

Six months later, I deployed my first site.

For me this marked the beginning of a hands-on approach to building for the web. During this time, I’ve been developing multiple sites while expanding my understanding of modern computer languages and workflows.

My interests sit at the intersection of design, accessibility, privacy, and social equity. I’m especially curious about how software, specifically web platforms and machine learning, can be used by and for marginalised communities, in the hopes of building healthier online spaces.

Currently, I’m exploring microblog-style platforms as an alternative to mainstream social media, while studying principals of computer science, graphic, UI and UX design. Eventually I want to focus on accessibility, encryption, and user privacy, something that has become increasingly important to me.

Recently, it seems like the internet is running low on platforms for people to socialise without this later being weaponised against them. This is something I'd like to see change, and I thought, why don't I learn how?

Still, with even my loftiest of ambitions, my approach is practical: build, learn, deploy, repeat. Each project helps me better understand not only how the internet works, but how it can work for us.
